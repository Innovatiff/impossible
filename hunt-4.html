🕵️ Confidential — Hunt 04

At 8:12 this morning, the housekeeper found Sir Arthur Pembroke dead on the floor of his drawing room — a letter opener at his side, the windows still letting in the June light.

The household insists no stranger came or went. His nephew was at the races. His former business partner was in the city with his lawyers. His secretary, Miss Margaret Calloway, swears she spent the whole morning shopping in town.

One of them is lying. The crime scene knows it, the clock knows it, and — once you find the key to his private study and unlock the computer on his desk — his own correspondence will say so plainly.

Examine everything. Trust the timestamps. When you are certain, name the killer.

— Chief Inspector's Office
